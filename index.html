What to do with your grounds

7 Brilliant
Things to Try!

From the garden to the bathroom cabinet — used coffee grounds are more versatile than you'd ever guess.

01

Supercharge your soil

Improves water retention, drainage, and aeration. Hydrangeas, roses, azaleas, and blueberries love them.

How to use Sprinkle lightly onto beds and pots.
02

Turbo-boost your compost

Used grounds are nitrogen-rich — exactly what your compost pile is craving. Think protein shake for your bin.

How to use Mix with dry "brown" materials like leaves, paper, or cardboard.
03

Keep pests at bay

Slugs, snails, and ants are not fans. Create a natural barrier and let your plants get on with growing.

How to use Scatter a thin ring of grounds around plant bases.
04

Brew "compost tea"

A simple overnight soak turns grounds into liquid fertiliser your indoor and outdoor plants will go wild for.

How to use 1–2 cups in water, leave overnight, water plants the next day.
05

Banish bad smells

Coffee grounds naturally absorb odours — a no-fuss fix for fridges, shoe cupboards, car interiors, and bins.

How to use Dry and place in an open container or small fabric pouch.
06

Eco-friendly scrub

That coarse texture is a natural scourer. Tough on grease, gentle on your conscience. No chemicals needed.

How to use Mix with dish soap to scrub pans, sinks, and outdoor surfaces.
07

Get creative

Candles, soaps, body scrubs, paper dye, homemade exfoliators — grounds add gorgeous colour, texture, and scent.

How to use Mix into your recipe or craft base at whatever quantity your project calls for.
